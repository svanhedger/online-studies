Online Experiments

If you are interested in getting a first-hand feel of my research program, I invite you to participate in a current research project. If you decide to participate in any of these experiments, please make sure that you are (1) in a quiet listening environment, (2) using headphones, and (3) have your device's volume unmuted and at turned to a comfortable listening level. Participation is completely voluntary and non-obligatory, meaning you may choose to quit at any point without penalty. You will receive feedback as to your performance at the end of each experiment, and your data will be kept anonymous and stored on a password-protected server.

We invite you to take part in a study on absolute pitch. In this study, you will label musical notes that you hear. You will also be asked to provide some basic demographic information, as well as some information about your language and musical experiences. This study takes around 15 minutes to complete. This experiment takes around 15 minutes to complete. On each trial, you will hear two versions of a familiar melody (e.g., a melody from a popular song). One version will be presented in the "correct" musical key (i.e., it will use the same collection of pitches as the most commonly heard recording of the melody outside of this experiment). The other version will be presented in an "incorrect" key (i.e., it will use a different collection of pitches from the most commonly heard recording of the melody outside of this experiment). Your task is to judge which melody version sounds "correct." You will be given feedback on how you performed at the end of the experiment.
