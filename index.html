About Me

I am currently a BrainsCAN Postdoctoral Fellow at Western University, working jointly with Ingrid Johnsrude and Laura Batterink. I completed my graduate work in the Department of Psychology at the University of Chicago, working with Howard Nusbaum.

In my research, I use behavioral and neuroscientific (fMRI, EEG) methodologies to understand the factors that influence auditory perception, learning, and memory. I have focused on music and speech as model systems for understanding (1) how prior experience shapes perception, (2) the limits of auditory plasticity in adulthood, and (3) how both implicit and explicit learning mechanisms contribute to auditory representations.

To learn more about my specific research projects, please click on my Research tab.
